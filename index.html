Topic 1.0

Psychology in Nursing

Understanding human behavior, mental processes, and their application in patient care

9 Episodes ~45 min read Updated June 2026 Psychology, Nursing Foundation
4/9 Episodes Complete 65% Mastery

Learning Objectives

By the end of this topic, you should be able to:

  • Define psychology and explain its relevance to nursing practice
  • Identify key psychological concepts and theories used in patient care
  • Apply psychological principles to assess and address patient needs
  • Recognize the psychological aspects of illness and hospitalization
  • Integrate psychological knowledge into holistic nursing care

1.1 Definition of Psychology

Understanding the scientific study of behavior and mental processes

1.1.1 Key Concepts

Psychology is the scientific study of behavior and mental processes. It encompasses everything from the biological underpinnings of our actions to the social and cultural influences that shape who we are.

In the context of nursing, psychology provides a framework for understanding how patients think, feel, and behave in response to illness, treatment, and the healthcare environment.

Behavior Any observable action, response, or activity of an organism
Cognition Mental processes including thinking, memory, perception, and problem-solving
Affect The experience and expression of emotion
Motivation The internal processes that initiate, guide, and maintain goal-directed behavior
Personality The unique pattern of thoughts, feelings, and behaviors that characterize an individual
Stress The body's response to demands or threats, affecting physical and mental health
Clinical Insight

Nurses apply psychology daily when assessing patient anxiety, understanding coping mechanisms, and identifying readiness for behavioral change. A patient's psychological state significantly impacts their physical recovery, treatment adherence, and overall health outcomes.

Level A Evidence

1.1.2 Nursing Relevance

Psychology is integral to nursing for several reasons:

  • Patient Assessment: Understanding psychological factors helps nurses conduct comprehensive assessments
  • Communication: Psychological knowledge enhances therapeutic communication skills
  • Behavior Change: Nurses use psychological principles to promote healthy behaviors
  • Stress Management: Recognizing and addressing patient stress improves outcomes
  • Emotional Support: Understanding emotional responses enables better patient support
Psychological concepts and their nursing applications
Psychological Concept Definition Nursing Application
Behavior Any observable action or response Assess patient compliance, activity level, and response to treatment
Cognition Mental processes of thinking and understanding Evaluate patient understanding of health information and decision-making capacity
Affect Emotional expression and experience Recognize signs of depression, anxiety, or emotional distress
Motivation Internal drives that guide behavior Identify factors that influence patient engagement in care
Personality Unique patterns of thoughts and behaviors Understand individual differences in patient responses to illness

1.2 Psychological Needs

Understanding and addressing the psychological needs of patients

1.2.1 Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs

Abraham Maslow's hierarchy of needs is a foundational theory in nursing psychology. It proposes that human needs are arranged in a hierarchy, with basic physiological needs at the base and self-actualization at the top.

For nurses, understanding this hierarchy is essential because higher-level needs cannot be effectively addressed until lower-level needs are met. A patient in pain (physiological need) will not respond well to education about lifestyle changes (self-esteem needs) until their pain is managed.

B Breathing Monitor
C Circulation Stable
Clinical Warning

Never assume that all patients have their basic needs met. Always assess for:

  • Pain and physical comfort
  • Safety concerns (falls, abuse, neglect)
  • Nutrition and hydration
  • Sleep and rest
  • Elimination needs

1.2.2 Patient Assessment of Psychological Needs

Effective nursing assessment of psychological needs involves observation, interview, and the use of standardized tools. Key areas to assess include:

  • Emotional State: Anxiety, depression, fear, anger, grief
  • Coping Mechanisms: Adaptive vs. maladaptive coping strategies
  • Support Systems: Family, friends, community resources
  • Stressors: Current sources of stress and their impact
  • Self-Esteem: Patient's perception of self-worth and body image
Nursing Tip

Use therapeutic communication techniques: Active listening, open-ended questions, and empathetic responses are essential tools for assessing psychological needs. Create a safe, non-judgmental environment where patients feel comfortable expressing their concerns.

1.3 Clinical Application

Applying psychological principles in nursing practice

Common Psychological Challenges in Patients

Patients experience a range of psychological challenges that nurses must recognize and address:

  • Anxiety: Related to diagnosis, treatment, unknown outcomes
  • Depression: Common in chronic illness, pain, and disability
  • Fear: Of pain, death, loss of independence, the unknown
  • Grief and Loss: Loss of health, function, identity, and life as it was
  • Anger and Frustration: Often directed at healthcare providers or family
  • Denial: A common initial response to serious diagnosis
  • Withdrawal: Social isolation and reduced communication

Nursing Interventions

Therapeutic Communication Active listening, empathy, open-ended questions, validation
Patient Education Information provision, health literacy, self-management strategies
Emotional Support Empathy, presence, validation of feelings, reassurance
Stress Management Relaxation techniques, coping strategies, referrals to support services
Advocacy Ensuring patient voices are heard, addressing concerns with the healthcare team
Multidisciplinary Collaboration Working with psychologists, social workers, and other healthcare providers
Case Scenario

Patient: A 45-year-old woman newly diagnosed with breast cancer. She expresses fear about treatment, concerns about body image, and anxiety about the impact on her family.

Nursing Actions:
1. Provide a safe space for her to express her feelings
2. Offer information about treatment options in understandable terms
3. Connect her with support groups and counseling services
4. Include family members in education and support
5. Collaborate with the multidisciplinary team (psychologist, social worker, oncology team)
6. Monitor for signs of depression or anxiety that may require referral

Key takeaways and self-assessment questions

Key Takeaways

  • Therapeutic communication is the foundation of psychological nursing care
